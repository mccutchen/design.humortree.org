United Way Agencies
American Red Cross

Gives help to victims of disaster; provides emergency communications between military personnel and their families; teaches first aid, CPR, swimming, lifeguarding, and other safety courses.

(903) 753-2091
The Arc of Gregg County
(Formerly Gregg County Association for Retarded Citizens)

Services include day care, social activities, physical education, Special Olympics, and educational enrichment programs. Counseling service for parents and family members is also available. Legal advocacy to the mentally disabled included.

(903) 753-0723
 
Because I Care

Educates the general public regarding the need for potential donors of bone marrow. It also recruits and tissue-types potential bone marrow donors.

(903) 535-5409  /  800-MARROW-2
 
Boy Scouts of America

Opportunity to build desirable qualities of character and mental and physical fitness. An effective program designed to develop self-esteem, leadership skills, personal resourcefulness, ability to get along with others, and better understanding and respect for American values.

(903) 597-7201
 
Boys & Girls Club of Gregg County

Builds good citizens through everyday leadership and guidance. Young people of all nationalities, races and creeds join in wholesome recreation and companionship.

(903) 234-9130
