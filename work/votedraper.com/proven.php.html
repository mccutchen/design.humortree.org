“Proven Performance”

* * *

During Charlene's tenure as a city councilwoman, Longview has been set on a course of economic viability; with the expectation of reducing or retiring the city's debt early and lowering the city's tax rate by a minimum of five cents by 2003. In addition, during this time the city has put together an effective management team, initiated the process of improving employees' salaries and benefits, while maintaining the level of service and commitment to the citizens of Longview.

In particular, District 2 has grown and been revitalized in such areas as:

  • S.L.I.P. (South Longview Incentive Program) - Designed to encourage and support building new homes and apartments in underdeveloped areas of the city
  • Improved infrastructure (i.e., 12th Street curb and gutter project)
  • Demolition of condemned properties
  • Opening of the Paula Jones Martin Recreation Center
  • Stamper Park improvements
  • Developed a quarter-mile walking track in collaboration with LISD
  • Assisted in negotiating an increase of major corporations' contributions to the city's budget
  • Sponsored town hall meetings while returning phone calls of citizens in District 2 and other citizens throughout the city
  • Encouraged companies to remain or locate new businesses on the south side of town; thus improving viability of the entire city
  • Worked to build affordable homes in underdeveloped areas of the city
